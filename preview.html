Chinos are great—not in high heat, though. And even chino shorts can feel too restrictive for the hottest days of the year. That’s why we made the Flint and Tinder Canoe Short, a hybrid design that deviates from traditional, overbearing bottoms without being too technical. By swapping thick-cut cotton for breathable cotton poplin, and a fixed waistband for an elasticated one, the shorts have enough structure to hold their shape without cutting into our waist or suffocating our thighs. Integrated stretch means better movement, and the 7-inch inseam hits right where it should for a clean, athletic look. They’re easy to throw on and hard to wear out.

Spanning some 16,000 acres at the foot of Colorado’s San Juan Mountains sits Ralph Lauren’s Double RL Ranch—part collector’s paradise, part creative base camp under the big sky. It’s the physical manifestation of his sub-brand, RRL, a repro project within Ralph Lauren that we’ve proudly featured for years. Drawing on vintage military, Native American, frontier, and American workwear designs, the spring restock features a few faithful remakes—like a breathable chambray shirt with a hand-sanded fade and cotton poplin cargo pants with functional surplus-style pockets ripped from 50s-era field gear.
